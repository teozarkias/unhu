Where the name comes from

I am, because we are.

Unhu is a Shona word — close cousin to "Ubuntu" — describing a person's humanity found through their relationships with others. No one becomes themselves alone.

That's the whole idea behind this room: long shared tables, coffee made to be lingered over, and a corner of Kallithea where regulars and strangers end up at the same table by the second visit.
